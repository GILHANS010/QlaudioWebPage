Instruments Included in the String Series

Ajaeng (Jungak)

A Korean traditional string instrument with a deep and profound tone, similar to the cello in Western music in terms of range and rich tone. The Jungak Ajaeng is larger and used primarily in court music. It's played with a bow made of forsythia wood and produces a uniquely deep, resonant sound that forms the foundation of many traditional Korean ensembles.

Ajaeng (Sanjo)

A smaller and more agile version of the Ajaeng than the Jungak Ajaeng, with an expressive tone similar to the higher range of the cello. The Sanjo Ajaeng is used in folk music and solo performances. Its smaller size allows for more intricate and faster playing techniques, making it suitable for the improvisational Sanjo style of music.

Gayageum (25 strings)

A modernly modified version of the traditional Gayageum, with a rich and elegant tone similar to the harp in Western music and a wider range. This contemporary adaptation features 25 strings instead of the traditional 12, allowing for a greater tonal range and the ability to play more complex harmonies. It's widely used in both traditional and fusion music styles.

Gayageum (Jungak)

A traditional Gayageum mainly used in court music, with a clear and elegant tone similar to the harp and characterized by refined performance. The Jungak Gayageum has 12 strings and is known for its gentle, sophisticated sound. It's typically played with a more restrained technique, emphasizing grace and subtlety in court and ceremonial music.

Gayageum (Sanjo)

A Gayageum mainly used in folk music, with a more intense and expressive tone than the harp, allowing for various playing techniques. The Sanjo Gayageum, also with 12 strings, is designed for more dynamic and emotionally expressive playing. It's capable of producing a wide range of tones and is often used in solo performances and improvisational music.

Geomungo

A representative Korean string instrument with a heavy and deep resonance, with a low and rich tone similar to the double bass in Western music. The Geomungo has six strings and is played with a short bamboo stick called a suldae. It's known for its powerful, resonant sound and is often used to provide a strong rhythmic and harmonic foundation in ensemble music.

Haegeum

A traditional Korean string instrument using a bow, with a range similar to the violin in Western music and capable of delicate and flexible expressions. The Haegeum has two strings and is held vertically when played. Its unique timbre can mimic human vocal qualities, making it exceptionally expressive. It's used in various genres of Korean music, from court music to contemporary compositions.

Yanggeum

A string instrument played by striking with bamboo sticks, with a clear and crystalline tone similar to the celesta in Western music and producing a unique resonance. The Yanggeum is a hammered dulcimer that was introduced to Korea from China. Its bright, sparkling tone adds a distinctive color to Korean ensembles and is often used to provide intricate melodic lines and ornamentation.
