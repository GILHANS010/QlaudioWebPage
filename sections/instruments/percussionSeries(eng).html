Instruments Included in the Percussion Series

Korean traditional percussion instruments, each offering distinct timbres and rich expressions. This series includes various percussion instruments such as the Banghyang and Pyeonjong, allowing users to experience the authentic sounds of traditional Korean music.

Included Instruments:

  • Banghyang:

    The Banghyang is a traditional Korean chime instrument that belongs to the mallet family. It consists of a series of metal or stone plates that are struck with mallets to produce sound. The Banghyang is known for its clear, bright tone, similar to the glockenspiel in Western music. It is often used in court music and ceremonial contexts, providing a shimmering, bell-like sound that enhances the melodic and harmonic richness of the ensemble.

  • Pyeongyeong:

    The Pyeongyeong is an ancient stone percussion instrument that originated in China and was adopted into Korean traditional music. It comprises a set of L-shaped stone slabs that are struck with a mallet to produce a clear, pure sound, much like the lithophone in Western music. The Pyeongyeong's tone is resonant and crystalline, making it ideal for creating serene and contemplative atmospheres in both ritual and court music.

  • Pyeonjong:

    The Pyeonjong is a bell-shaped percussion instrument made of bronze. It features a set of suspended bells that are struck with a mallet to produce a deep and rich resonance. The sound of the Pyeonjong is similar to that of chime bells in Western music, offering a profound and majestic quality. It is traditionally used in court music and various ceremonial performances, adding a grand and solemn tone to the ensemble.

  • Samulnori Set:

    Samulnori is a genre of traditional Korean percussion music that is performed with a set of four instruments: the Kkwaenggwari (small gong), Buk (barrel drum), Janggu (hourglass drum), and Jing (large gong). This set perfectly captures the dynamism and rhythm of Korean traditional music, with each instrument playing a distinct role:

    Kkwaenggwari: Produces a sharp, piercing sound that leads the ensemble and adds rhythmic accents.

    Buk: Provides a deep, resonant bass tone that forms the rhythmic foundation.

    Janggu: Offers a versatile range of sounds, from deep bass to high-pitched tones, allowing for complex rhythms and expressive playing.

    Jing: Produces a deep, resonant tone that adds a powerful and majestic quality to the music.

    The Samulnori set is often used in festivals, rituals, and folk performances, creating a vibrant and energetic musical experience.

  • Cinematic Percussion Set:

    The Cinematic Percussion Set is a large-scale collection of percussion instruments designed for film music and various genres of music production. This set includes 17 different Cinematic Percussion Kits, each offering unique sounds and textures that are reminiscent of epic percussion and taiko drums. The instruments in this set include:

    Geongo: A large drum with a deep, booming sound.

    Jingo: A medium-sized drum with a rich, resonant tone.

    Gyobanggo: A smaller drum with a sharp, clear sound.

    Jwago: A double-headed drum with a versatile range of tones.

    Nogo: A barrel drum with a deep, powerful sound.

    Jeolgo: A drum with a bright, crisp tone.

    Eunggo: A large drum with a majestic, resonant sound.

    Saggo: A smaller drum with a high-pitched, penetrating tone.

    Yonggo: A dragon drum with a deep, rolling sound.

    Yeonggo: A ceremonial drum with a grand, powerful tone.

    Bak: Wooden clappers that produce a sharp, percussive sound.

    Bara: Cymbals that create a bright, metallic crash.

    Bu: A gong that produces a deep, resonant tone.

    Chuk: A wooden box that produces a hollow, resonant sound when struck.

    Eo: A tiger-shaped wooden scraper that creates a rasping sound.

    Nodo: A bamboo clapper that produces a crisp, clicking sound.

    Yeongdo: A bell that creates a clear, ringing tone.

Why Decent Sampler?

We chose Decent Sampler for the following reasons:

  • High accessibility: Decent Sampler is provided for free, making it highly accessible to both users and developers.
  • Optimized software: Decent Sampler uses less RAM, efficiently using system resources.
  • Expandability: It allows for adding various functions and tones, enabling customized instrument settings.

For these reasons, we chose Decent Sampler to provide users with the best experience. We hope that through the advantages of Decent Sampler, more people can easily access the beauty of Korean traditional instruments.
